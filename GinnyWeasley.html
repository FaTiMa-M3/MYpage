Ginny Weasley


“Because – oh shut up laughing, you two – because they’ve both just been turned down by girls they asked to the ball!” - Ginny Weasley

Ginny Weasley is the youngest Weasley sibling and only daughter in her family. The movies forget she is a seasoned quidditch player and a fierce rebel.
But is that all Ginny Weasley is or is there more to her romantisized character?

Rating:6/10

Ginny has flaws that are pushed down like Hermione and doesn't face consquences for her actions. Some of her flaws are:
  • Violent. While I wouldn't say she is vindictve, she certainly over-reacts in certain situations (hexes Zacharias Smith, crashes into quidditch
    commentarry post)
  • She has a sharp tongue (calls Ron a 12 year old and tells him Hermione kissed Victor Krum when its perfectly obvious they like eachother)
  • Hypocrite (She calls Luna 'Looney' but tells others off for doing so)
    • This, paired with the fact that everything she does is romantisized, and she becomes a very annoying character compared to the one we know. She is
    soley wrtten as Harry's love intrest but doesn't get the proper character arc necessary. Her mean acts is written as savage and sassy with Harry
    sticking up for her because he is in love with her. I think if it was anyone else doing this, Harry would definitely scold them.

    Ginny stills has her good traits of course, here are some of them:
  • Loyal (She’s one of the only Dumbledore’s Army members who helps the trio go to the Ministry to try to rescue Sirius)
  • A leader (Along with Luna and Neville, she starts up Dumbledore’s Army while Voldemort has control of the school)
  • Talented (her Bat-bogey hex)
  • Independent (Learned how to play quidditch all by herself)
  • Brave (Fought in the battle of Hogwarts, she starts up Dumbledore’s Army while Voldemort has control of the school, one of the only Dumbledore’s
    Army members who helps the trio go to the Ministry to try to rescue Sirius)
    • She also witty, kind and energetic. Ginny is a free, brave spirit and I like that she defies the stereotypes.
